About The Overtones

Kansas City's big band since 1997

Why Live Music Makes the Difference

A playlist can fill a room with sound. A live big band fills a room with energy. There's something that happens when sixteen musicians lock into a groove together — a shared momentum that a recording simply can't replicate. You feel it in your chest before you hear it with your ears.

At a wedding, it's the moment the dance floor fills. At a corporate gala, it's when the room stops networking and starts listening. At a festival, it's when strangers start dancing next to each other. Live music creates shared moments. That's what we've been doing since 1997 — and it's what we love about this.

🎷 Carrying on a Kansas City Tradition

Kansas City has always been a jazz town. In the 1930s and '40s, KC was one of the hotbeds of American jazz — a place where Count Basie developed his signature swing style, and where bandleader Jay McShann gave a young Charlie Parker his first real break. The legendary 12th & Vine district pulsed with the sound of big bands night after night.

That tradition runs deep. The Overtones of Kansas City see themselves as part of that lineage — sixteen musicians keeping the big band sound alive and bringing it to new audiences, new venues, and new generations.

The Band Today

The Overtones of Kansas City is a 16-piece ensemble featuring five saxophones, six trombones, four trumpets, and a rhythm section of piano, bass, and drums. [Anything worth adding about the current band's character or personality? — ask Larry or Catherine]

We've performed for weddings, anniversaries, city festivals, corporate dinners, fundraisers, and veteran appreciation events. Whether your guests want to dance all night or simply enjoy the music, we can tailor the performance to the moment.

Regardless of your age or musical tastes — if you like big band at all, you will love The Overtones.
