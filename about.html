About The Overtones

Kansas City's big band since 1997

🎷 Carrying on a Kansas City Tradition

Kansas City has always been a jazz town. In the 1930s and '40s, KC was one of the hotbeds of American jazz — home to Count Basie and the club where Jay McShann gave a young Charlie Parker his first real break.

The Overtones see themselves as part of that lineage — sixteen musicians keeping the big band sound alive and bringing it to new audiences and new generations.

Why Live Music Makes the Difference

A playlist can fill a room with sound. A live big band fills a room with energy. There's something that happens when all the musicians lock into a groove together — a shared momentum that a recording simply can't replicate.

At a wedding, it's the moment the dance floor fills. At a corporate gala, it's when the room stops networking and starts listening. Live music creates shared moments. That's what we've been doing since 1997.

On the Name "The Overtones"

Every musical note is more than a single pitch. When a player sounds a note, the vibrating air column produces a cascade of higher frequencies above it — called overtones. Those overtones give brass instruments their characteristic sound. Saxophone players shape the same harmonic energy through the reed and body of the horn.

Our Story

The Overtones of Kansas City formed in 1997, when Sheree Shilt inherited her father's huge library of big band music and started a small combo including John Byrne, Mary Ring, and Sheree Shilt, for the First United Methodist Church in Kearney, Missouri. From that seed she assembled the musicians for a full traditional big band. The Overtones quickly took the music beyond the church walls — playing North Metro Mall, Smithville Park, the Gladstone Festival, and even bringing Christmas music to the lingerie department of Walmart.

Over the years, players have come and gone, but the passion for the music has remained constant. For many years, Jim Leuschke served as Music Director, building the band's repertoire and shaping its sound. He passed the baton to Larry Fitzpatrick, who leads the band today. The library has grown to include more than 180 songs — spanning everything from Glenn Miller swing to Earth, Wind & Fire — while the core commitment to great live music has never wavered.

Then came COVID-19. Everything shut down, and the Overtones went silent in March 2020. Recovery was slow and uncertain. In the summer of 2020, Catherine asked the band if there was any way to keep playing — and Jim came up with Kearney Park. So until it got too cold, the Overtones entertained park participants outdoors, keeping the music alive during an otherwise quiet time. In the spring of 2021, the band played its first real post-COVID gig: an 80th birthday and 25th anniversary celebration. It was a welcome return to what they love.

But the hiatus also brought opportunity. When the band came back together, it did so with fresh energy and several talented new players joining the roster. New voices, new charts added to the library, and new venues opened their doors to the sound of the Overtones. The return to the stage felt less like picking up where things left off and more like a second beginning.

The Band Today

The Overtones of Kansas City consists of a full big band ensemble, including saxophone, trumpet, trombone sections, and a rhythm section of piano, bass, and drums.

We've performed for weddings, anniversaries, city festivals, corporate dinners, fundraisers, and veteran appreciation events. Whether your guests want to dance all night or simply enjoy the music, we can tailor the performance to the moment by blending music from multiple genres, including traditional jazz, swing, dance, rock, blues, folk, funk, Latin, pop, and patriotic songs.

Regardless of your age or musical tastes — if you like big band music at all, you will love The Overtones.
