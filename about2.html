About The Overtones

Kansas City's big band since 1997

On the Name "The Overtones"

Every musical note is more than a single pitch. When a player sounds a note, the vibrating air column produces a cascade of higher frequencies above it — called overtones. Those overtones give brass instruments their characteristic sound. Saxophone players shape the same harmonic energy through the reed and body of the horn.

Our Story

The Overtones of Kansas City formed in 1997, when Sheree Shilt inherited her father's huge library of big band music and started a small combo including John Byrne, Mary Ring, and Sheree Shilt, for the First United Methodist Church in Kearney, Missouri. From that seed she assembled the musicians for a full traditional big band.

Over the years, players have come and gone, but the passion for the music has remained constant. Larry Fitzpatrick leads the band today. The library has grown to include more than 180 songs — spanning everything from Glenn Miller swing to Earth, Wind & Fire.

Then came COVID-19. In the summer of 2020, Catherine asked if there was any way to keep playing — and Jim came up with Kearney Park. The band entertained park participants outdoors until it got too cold. In the spring of 2021, the band played its first real post-COVID gig: an 80th birthday and 25th anniversary celebration.

  • 1997
    Band started as a combo for First United Methodist Church in Kearney, MO
  • 1998–2001
    Transformed into a big band with additional musicians
  • 2000s
    Performances at Metro North Mall, Smithville Park, Gladstone Festival, and Walmart
  • 2020
    COVID-19 pause, but continued playing outside at Kearney Park
  • 2021
    Return to the stage with an 80th birthday and 25th anniversary celebration
  • TODAY
    Up to 16 musicians, 180+ songs, performing across the Kansas City area

🎷 Carrying on a Kansas City Tradition

Kansas City has always been a jazz town. In the 1930s and '40s, KC was one of the hotbeds of American jazz — a place where Count Basie developed his signature swing style, and where bandleader Jay McShann gave a young Charlie Parker his first real break.

The Overtones of Kansas City see themselves as part of that lineage — sixteen musicians keeping the big band sound alive and bringing it to new audiences, new venues, and new generations.

The Band Today

The Overtones consists of a full big band ensemble — saxophones, trumpets, trombones, and a rhythm section of piano, bass, and drums.

We've performed for weddings, anniversaries, city festivals, corporate dinners, fundraisers, and veteran appreciation events. We can tailor the performance to the moment by blending music from jazz, swing, rock, blues, funk, Latin, pop, and patriotic songs.

Regardless of your age or musical tastes — if you like big band music at all, you will love The Overtones.

🎺 Join the Band

The Overtones are always looking for talented musicians. Whether you want a permanent chair or just want to sit in for a rehearsal, you're welcome to come and play.

Current open positions:

  • Trombone
  • Tenor Saxophone
  • Vocalist (for select charts)

Contact Larry Fitzpatrick at overtonesofkc@gmail.com.

Why Live Music Makes the Difference

A playlist can fill a room with sound. A live big band fills a room with energy. There's something that happens when all the musicians lock into a groove together — a shared momentum that a recording simply can't replicate.

At a wedding, it's the moment the dance floor fills. At a corporate gala, it's when the room stops networking and starts listening. Live music creates shared moments. That's what we've been doing since 1997.
